FATHER
August Selch [Selch-Weiss]
Born: 01/01/1874 in RUS,
Married: Amalia (Oma) Weiss-Selch [Selch-Weiss] in 04/20/1900
Occupation: Town Scribe
Currently Inhabits Beausejour,MB,R0E 0C0,CAN
Died: 06/27/1941
Life Story: Was one of the founders of St. Paul's Lutheran Church in Green Bay, MB 1898. Married Amalia Weise 04/20/1900 who arrived from Europe. They met and were married in Winnipeg by Pastor F. Beer. In 1917 they moved to a farm on the east side of Beausejour, but the Green Bay property was kept for grain. They started a dairy business in 1928. Initially all the cows were milked by hand, and the milk, in 8 gallon cans had to be hauled by horse-drawn wagon to the railway station in Beausejour, to be shipped to Winnipeg. By 1935 the milk was picked up by a transfer truck and hauled to the city. August Selch was the first to own a cream separator.

Life Story: Immigrated with wife Wilhelmine from Russia in 1892. bringing August, Paulina, Mary, Theophilia; settling first in Winnipeg. In 1896 August Selch took a homestead on 16-13-8E in Green Bay, MB. Christian settled with them. The homestead was a dense forest. With the help of a team of oxen they began to clear the land. Soon they also acquired a horse. The horse, teamed up with the one owned by the neighbouring Litke family enabled the 2 families to make trips to Beausejour for supplies.
